# Origin

In the summer of 1998, I was an intern at [Ubilab](https://www.ubilab.org/) (and [found myself in good company](https://www.ubilab.org/people/index.html)).

My internship assignment was to develop a prototype for *secure distributed deposits* (in Java). The assignment was motivated by the idea that banks such as UBS might one day offer their customers not only physcial but also digital vaults. The mathematical/cryptographic foundation for digital vaults had been established by [*Shamir's Secret Sharing*](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/359168.359176).

It took over twenty years before I revisited the topic, even though I myself had repeatedly encountered a need for something like Deposplit in the interim. In March 2022, I summarized my idea in [two pages](https://github.com/Deposplit/deposplit.com/blob/main/SecretSharingApp.pdf) and subsequently shared it with others from time to time (for instance, when professors asked me for student project ideas). However, no one ever took the bait so it took another four years before I laid the groundwork for Deposplit (together with [Claude Code](https://www.anthropic.com/product/claude-code)).
